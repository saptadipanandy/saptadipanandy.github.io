Mapping on GIS

 A Picture is worth a Thousand Words. A Map is worth even more. 

Here are some stories that the map of India tells.

Croplands

The green colour represents the croplands of India. They comprise 54% of all land.

Source: GLCM2000 (30 sec resolution), GADM v1.0 
Made with ArcGIS

Population Density

Each district’s color represents the population density of that district.

Source: CIESIN 2000 (30 seconds resolution), GADM v1.0 
Made with ArcGIS

Altitude

Each district’s color represents the highest altitude in that district.

Source: CGIAR SRTM (3 seconds resolution), GADM v1.0 
Made with ArcGIS

Land Cover

The different colours represent different land covers like tree cover, croplands, water bodies, built up areas etc.

Source: GLCM2000 (30 sec resolution), GADM v1.0 
Made with ArcGIS

Urban Areas

The yellow spots are the visible urban areas i.e. artificially built up spaces. It comprises 0.64% of the total land

Source: GLCM2000 (30 sec resolution), GADM v1.0 
Made with ArcGIS

Railway Stations

The map shows number of railway stations in each state of India. This includes suburban railway stations as well.

Source: Sajjad Anwar, Sanjay Bhangar, GADM 
Made with ArcGIS
