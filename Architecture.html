National Museum of Architecture

Under-graduation Thesis

Nature of work

Museum Design

Purpose of work

To create a sense of place & space that will educate, inspire, engage and connect common man with Indian Architecture

Duration

6 months

Brief Summary

In order to evoke the sense of place and space that inspires and engages the individual, it was essential to design the museum in a way that the architecture of the building form itself speaks to the visitor about the rich history of Indian architecture. This was achieved by the methodical approach of: 
▷ Categorization of different styles of architecture through time to meet the functional requirement of creating different gallery spaces.
▷ Maximization of the degree of interaction through built form
▷ Creation of pedestrian experience restricting vehicular movement
▷ Design of spaces which by itself would demonstrate the architecture being showcased
▷ Integration of modern technologies and materials to showcase the architecture of past

Mapping Ephemerality

Research project

Nature of work

Mapping and documentation

Purpose of work

To understand the relation between the 'ephemeral' and the 'permanent' in the production of cities.

Duration

3 months

Brief Summary

This was a multi-phased project, which was conducted in 2018 by University of Santa Barbara. In the first phase, a study of Durgapuja in Kolkata was done, using Durgapuja as an entry point into the questions of Kolkata's urban legacy.

The work included on-site documentation, using both analog and digital technologies, preparing maps and presenting written and graphical information. For analyzing the relation between the ephemeral and the permanent, a host of methods for data collection and analysis was used. The task entailed using GIS technology in the field, photographic and architectural documentation, and often some interviewing.

The second phase included detailed documentation of 16 puja pandals chosen on the basis of physical, urban and historical context. This documentation process included architectural drawings of pandals, written reports on context and recording of ideas and conception of locals about that locality's puja.

Community Centre

Academic Studio Project

Nature of work

Community Centre Design

Purpose of work

To design with the surrounding urban context forming the backbone of the design philosophy.

Duration

6 months

Brief Summary

The objective of this design studio curriculum was to design with contextualization. The selected site was at Panihati, a historically and culturally rich area north of Kolkata..

The first phase of the work included detailed on-site documentation, development of accurate working drawings of the site and it's surroundings, understanding the historical and cultural background of the area. 

The second phase of the work included conceptualization of the design philosophy, understanding the design constraints such as working with the site vegetation instead of against it, designing the community centre and finally creating a model depicting a faithful representation of the design.
