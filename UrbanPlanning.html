Urban Planning Projects

Preparation of Layout Plans of 3 Unauthorized Colonies

Consultancy Project for Delhi Development Authority

Nature of work

Layout Planning

Purpose of work

Regularization and Redevelopment of Unauthorized Colonies in Delhi

Duration

18 months

Brief Summary

The aim of this project was to prepare layout plans for regularization and redevelopment of 3 unauthorized colonies in Delhi, namely Khirki Extension (26 Ha), Saroop Nagar (34 Ha) and East Azad Nagar (1 Ha).

The work included:
▷ Site visits and discussion with stake-holders from the respective colonies.
▷ Preparation of Base Map (on ArcGIS) consisting of land use pattern, built form, road network, physical and social infrastructure facilities.
▷ Analysis of the present status and identification of problems and potentials.
▷ Preparation of Layout Plans (on ArcGIS) and proposals comprising of land use and infrastructure.
▷ Preparation of regularization and redevelopment strategies for the colonies and their governance for implementation.
▷ Assessment of financial feasibility for redevelopment of the colonies.

Purpose of work

Planning for in-situ slum redevelopment
through community engagement; Kolkata

Post-graduation Thesis

Nature of work

Slum redevelopment planning

Purpose of work

Improvement of living conditions of slum dwellers through community participation

Duration

12 months

Brief Summary

A common challenge for almost all cities in developing economies is providing housing and facilities for the low-income population mostly residing in slums. As a solution to this, many cities have opted for slum rehabilitation housing. While slum rehabilitation has its own advantages and disadvantages, most of which have been extensively studied and documented, comparatively no studies can be found discussing the role of community participation in slum redevelopment. The thesis aims to methodically analyze and arrive at the relevance and necessity of community engagement in in-situ slum redevelopment. First of all, an in-depth analysis on the existing in-situ redevelopment policy under Housing for all (2015) has been done followed by identifying the existing role of community participation in the process. Next, a study has been done on the indicators of community participation from the existing literature on the subject. An empirical analysis of issues faced by slum dwellers after slum rehabilitation has also been done. Finally, national and international case studies were done to understand the relation between in situ slum redevelopment and community participation and how community participation can play a key role in solving the identified issues.
